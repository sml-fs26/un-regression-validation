# BRAINSTORM.md — `un-regression-validation`

## How to read this document

Stage 0 produced this. Stage 1 (Design Convergence) must, for each chapter, either *adopt* one of the wow-moments below or *document why every candidate was rejected*. The Devil's Advocate's standing critiques (final section) are binding constraints on Stage 2 implementation.

The ten-round brainstorm converged on a single architectural commitment: **the site is a typeset spreadsheet (`# five_deaths_of_norway.ipynb`) whose every chapter's signature image is a transformation of cells the reader owns or authored**. The scroll is the tutorial; the recast bar is the deployment. Chapter titles are retired in favor of **the five deaths of a protagonist cast by the data** (Norway, by audition on `h_ii_starkness`). Read this document as a spec for that artifact, not a set of alternatives.

## Cross-cutting visual signatures

The committee converged on the following recurring vocabulary that should run through *every* chapter:

1. **The `h_ii` heartbeat** — A single 9pt JetBrains Mono cell in the top-right viewport chrome, always visible, reading `<ISO>.h_ii = <value>`, captioned once in CH1 with a 7pt line: *"a country's share of her own prediction."* Climbs from `0.04` to `0.98` in CH2, contracts to `0.31` in CH3, dissolves into its own bootstrap interval `[0.18, 0.89]` in CH5. The biography compresses to one scalar. · *originated R6 Info Designer; promoted to heartbeat in R7/R8 Devil's Advocate; sealed R9/R10 all four agents.*

2. **The filename-as-title** — `# five_deaths_of_norway.ipynb` in 11pt JetBrains Mono at the top-left of the CSV at first paint. No title spread. No 240pt DM Serif gold. No page-fold. The title rewrites on recast (`# five_deaths_of_uruguay.ipynb`). The publisher is the dataset. · *originated R4 Info Designer + R4 Pedagogue; forced by R7 Devil's Advocate KILL of Aesthete's title spread.*

3. **The editable `role` column** — Pinned at CSV position 0; 30 cells carry `[authored]` pre-labels with hover-revealed citations; 326 cells start `unlabeled`. The reader tags during CH1 queries; every chart's role-stroke subscribes reactively in <300ms. The counter `labeled_by_you: N / 356` is always visible. CH5's scorecard reads from this column, not from authorial fiat. · *originated R6 Devil's Advocate ESCALATE ("defend the labels or hand them over"); committed R7–R9 across all four agents.*

4. **The protagonist cast by audition** — Before any chart is drawn, all 254 countries are scored on `max(h_ii(p))` over p ∈ [10, n]; the winner is the site's protagonist. Disclosed as one hairline mono line in the CSV gutter at first paint: `# cast: NOR (h_ii_starkness = 0.91). runners-up at /audition.` The `/audition` notebook is public and runnable. If Uruguay wins, the site retitles; the typography does not flinch. · *originated R4 Devil's Advocate AMPLIFY; sealed by R7 AMPLIFY + R8 KILL of the intermission dissolve.*

5. **The reader-authored palette (stroke-only)** — Role-colors (green/red/gray) appear ONLY as 1px strokes (underlines beneath feature names, outlines on country dots, hairline brackets). No fills. The single allowed exception is CH3's PI-cell decomposition, whose role-colored underlines carry the chapter's pedagogy. The strokes are the reader's own CH1 tags from second one. · *originated R1 Aesthete + R2 Aesthete "outline, never fill"; promoted to reader-authored in R6–R7.*

6. **Typography rationed exactly** — 11pt JetBrains Mono Medium for the model's voice (β̂ row), 11pt JetBrains Mono Regular for the data, 9pt mono for annotations and brackets, 14pt DM Serif Display small caps for CSV column headers. DM Serif Display italic appears **exactly**: 5 chapter interjections (32pt, one per chapter *except CH4, which is voiceless by design*) + one 48pt closing aphorism. Every serif appearance is accounted for. No 180pt cinematic numeral. No brass. No vellum. · *originated R1 Aesthete; sealed R8–R10 by Devil's Advocate KILLs of every staged artifact.*

7. **Every interaction is a cell edit** — Two scrubbable numeric cells (`p` in CH2, `α` in CH3); one editable column (`role`); four discrete gestures (double-click commit CH1, `[×]` objection CH4, drag-to-JOIN CH4, click-to-sort CH5); one text input (`recast` bar CH5). No sliders. No knobs. No painted maps. No dials. · *originated R4 Interaction Designer "every interaction is a cell edit"; sealed R9/R10.*

8. **Emit, do not perform** — No typewriter animation, no blinking cursor, no brass ticks, no 1.4s pacing, no staggered fade-ins. The β̂ row appears in one frame. The PI cell two-layer composition appears in one frame. The Dissolution's paragraph grows on the reader's scroll. The single allowed sub-200ms motion is: (a) the CH2 `h_ii` gold pulse at 0.95, (b) on-hover role-stroke reveal (CH4), (c) strikethrough draws on α-scrub (CH3, 120ms). Everything else is either layout consequence or cell state change. · *originated R5 Devil's Advocate KILL of cinematic confession staging; sealed R6–R9.*

9. **Norway's row is sticky-gold across all five chapters** — Her ISO-3 in the gutter, her five diagnostic cells (`gdp_actual | gdp_predicted | pi_95% | h_ii | mae_across_splits`) render the chapter's current state. Every chapter's signature frame floats above this row. At any chapter boundary, the reader can screenshot Norway's row and read where they are in the biography. · *originated R1 Interaction Designer + R1 Aesthete country-dot motif; promoted to structural biography in R4 Devil's Advocate AMPLIFY; committed R5+.*

10. **The scroll → recast-bar handoff** — The scroll is the tutorial that trains a reader to read the re-performed biography the recast bar deploys. A reader who finishes Norway and types `MNG` reads Mongolia's deaths in the grammar they just learned. Build-time enforcement: a reader who lands directly on `/v-one-draw` sees `# the recast bar requires the tutorial. scroll from the top.` — not a tooltip, a refusal. · *originated R5 Devil's Advocate ESCALATE ("why scrollytelling"); answered R7 Info Designer; sealed R9.*

## Chapter 1 — *Norway is indistinguishable from McDonald's*

**Job of this chapter:** Establish the protagonist, the reader's complicity in correlation-fishing, and the first whispered confession — all without a bar chart. Set up the `h_ii` ticker that will carry the biography.

### Top wow-moments (ranked)

#### 1. ★★★ The heartbeat cell, captioned
- **What:** Top-right of the first viewport, 9pt JetBrains Mono in the chrome: `NOR.h_ii = 0.04`. Beneath it, flush, 7pt mono: *a country's share of her own prediction.* That cell-pair is CH1's screenshot. The caption is the single most important sentence in the site; it appears *only* at CH1 first paint and fades after first scroll-through while the ticker alone persists. Every mechanism in CH1 (six queries, ten role-tags, the A/B commit, the first whispered bracket) is the reader earning the right to watch this cell climb in CH2.
- **Why it works:** The site's entire thesis compresses to one scalar with one caption. The reader who remembers nothing else remembers a number in a corner that they will see again, different, at the end of CH2. Eight characters plus a gloss. Perceptual lever: peripheral priming for a scalar the next chapter will weaponize.
- **Originated:** R6 Info Designer (leverage as sticky cell); promoted to heartbeat R7/R8 Devil's Advocate AMPLIFY; committed as CH1 signature R9 Devil's Advocate AMPLIFY; accepted by all four agents R10.
- **Implementation prerequisites:** The 7pt sub-caption must be legible beside a 9pt primary cell — verify at every viewport width and mobile. The ticker cell is rendered in viewport chrome (not document flow), so first-paint blocks on one numeric value, not on the full CSV bundle. The hat-matrix definition (`h_ii is the i-th diagonal of H = X(X'X)⁻¹X'`) is available on hover but never surfaced by default.

#### 2. ★★ The reader-drawn ECDF, collapsed into the dataset's full null
- **What:** After the reader types (or clicks) their sixth column into the query bar — each query dropping a labeled tick onto a horizontal |r| axis in the reader's own role-palette — the query bar dims and the strip **expands to full-viewport for 2 seconds**. Six ticks float, each labeled in 9pt mono with the feature name the reader typed. A dashed-gray hairline null-band shades the floor. Then the 350 unqueried columns rain in, one tick per frame over 1.2s; when the rain settles, the reader's six are indistinguishable from the 350. One caption: `you built this in thirty seconds. 186 of 356 features would have fit inside it.`
- **Why it works:** The reader authored the indictment. Tick labels = their queries. Stroke colors = their tags. Null-band = cited permutation output. They cannot blame the authors because every pixel is something they typed or assigned. Narrative lever: complicity before pedagogy.
- **Originated:** R1 Info Designer ECDF overlay; R1 Pedagogue "which one would you bet your PhD on"; fused R3 cross-pollination; committed as CH1 scar R8 Devil's Advocate ESCALATE + R9 all four agents (Interaction Designer kept it as scar when Info Designer conceded it to the ticker).
- **Implementation prerequisites:** Six-query minimum; fewer queries get a grayer, smaller frame with honest copy. The `186 / 356` number is real permutation output and must be computed per dataset. Autocomplete must seed absurd features alongside serious ones (first-hint ghost suggestion after query 2). Reduced-motion shows the full frame immediately after the sixth query's reveal.

#### 3. ★ The role-tagging commit, pinning a suspect for five chapters
- **What:** An A/B forced choice on Norway's own values: `column_A[NOR] = 9.4` vs `column_B[NOR] = 14.0`, both returning r = 0.78 against log-GDP. The reader double-clicks the one they'd stake Norway's wealth on. On reveal, both names fill in; the unchosen column gets a `◌` pin in its header (persisting through CH2's buckle, CH3's strikethrough survivors, CH4's JOIN result, CH5's bracketed confession). The reader is then invited to tag each column's role (`causal | spurious | incidental`) via a three-way toggle; their tags become the site's palette everywhere. One second later, the first β̂-row whisper emits — specifically about the column they pinned: `> coef_[<pinned_column>] = +0.71   [95% CI: +0.63, +0.78]`. Flat emission. No cursor. No tick. Bracket calibrated from line one.
- **Why it works:** The scroll captures a prior *before* the evidence arrives. CH4's indictment will be about features the reader themselves flagged, and CH5's scorecard grades their tags against bootstrap CIs. Gestural lever: commitment is irrevocable within session — no undo — which is the pedagogical analog of the statistical pitfall the site teaches.
- **Originated:** R1 Pedagogue "bet your PhD"; R1 Interaction Designer "guess-then-reveal slider"; fused R2/R3 with Aesthete's commitment drawer; bound to the `role` column R7.
- **Implementation prerequisites:** Both branches (right pick / wrong pick) must be written — the pin becomes `✓` or `✗` and CH4's redemption copy changes accordingly. Two columns must truly match in shape at display size — verified in real data. Pin persists via session state across chapter transitions.

## Chapter 2 — *Norway's prediction explodes*

**Job of this chapter:** Render overfitting as the dataset's own typographic failure, with the `h_ii` ticker as the one legible cell explaining why. No hockey stick. No piano keys. No metaphor.

### Top wow-moments (ranked)

#### 1. ★★★ The CSV buckles under its own width
- **What:** The reader scrubs the `p` cell (click-and-drag on a numeric cell — spreadsheet-native scrub gesture). Columns append rightward; the β̂ row's values march `+0.31` → `+847.3` → `+2.3e4` → `+3.7e18` → `+Infinity`. Cells overflow their column borders; digits collide with neighboring headers; scientific-notation tails trail off-screen into the indigo margin. Norway's `pi_95%` cell renders literally `[-Infinity, +Infinity]`. Dashed-red hairlines mark breached cell boundaries (visual intent, not bug). At p > n the β̂ row contains `[Infinity, NaN, -Infinity, 1.2e18, undefined, NaN, …]`. **There is no second chart.** A 9pt mono pinned cell in the corner reads `log(λ_min(XᵀX)) = -34.7` — the one diagnostic permitted as the honest mechanism. Above the ruin, the chapter's sole DM Serif italic line, 14pt: *you are looking at the screenshot every analyst has taken and deleted.*
- **Why it works:** First non-metaphorical rendering of overfitting in the genre. Every analyst has opened a too-wide CSV at 2am and felt the shame the chapter is supposed to teach. The interface's own failure mode is the pedagogy. Aesthetic lever: typographic catastrophe composed with the same discipline as every other frame. Narrative lever: the reader's hand on the scroll-handle is the hand on the crash.
- **Originated:** R4 Interaction Designer "Column-Adding Gesture" / "CSV That Fights You"; amplified R5 Devil's Advocate AMPLIFY "push the CSV-overflow all the way"; committed R6 across all four agents.
- **Implementation prerequisites:** Overflow must be *deliberately* composed (CSS-variable-driven) so it feels like intent, not a layout bug. `Infinity`, `NaN`, and `+3.71e17` are real Python outputs at p=n; do not soften. `[object Object]` is a high-risk render — ship only with tasteful discipline or cut. Reduced-motion shows three pinned snapshots (p=50, p=n, p=356). Precompute β̂ at every integer p.

#### 2. ★★ The heartbeat climbs to 0.98; Norway's row is last to fail
- **What:** The ticker (top-right, 9pt mono) updates live: `NOR.h_ii = 0.04 → 0.12 → 0.41 → 0.83 → 0.98`. A single gold pulse flashes when it crosses 0.95 — the only animation permitted in the chapter. Norway's sticky row is the **last typographic structure to become illegible**; at p=n−3, her `gdp_predicted` digits collide with her `pi_95%` cell's `[−∞` and the reader cannot parse which number belongs to which cell. Every other cell has already failed. **The one cell that stays pristine is the ticker.** One 11pt mono line beneath the ruin: `at p = n−1, Norway is 98% of her own prediction. everything else in this viewport stopped being honest thirty seconds ago.` The TOC updates: `II. Norway ∈ [−∞, +∞]` — sub-line, 9pt mono: `h_ii = 0.98`.
- **Why it works:** Promotes `h_ii` from diagnostic to explanation. When every legible thing on screen fails and one 8-character cell still reads clearly, the reader knows exactly which number to trust and which to fear. *"98% of her own prediction"* is the tweet-sized takeaway for overfitting. Perceptual lever: legibility-against-illegibility contrast. No explainer in the field has taught leverage this way.
- **Originated:** R6 Info Designer "Norway's Row Has Its Own Leverage Number"; promoted to heartbeat R7/R8 Devil's Advocate AMPLIFY; committed across all four agents R8–R10.
- **Implementation prerequisites:** `h_ii(p)` trajectory for Norway must be monotonic toward 1 and verified at integer p values (hat-matrix diagonal computation). Tear-order of column failures must genuinely place Norway's row last — high-leverage observations hold longest, which is real. Ticker must be fixed-position in viewport chrome (not in scroll flow); never occluded during horizontal scroll. Mobile: ticker collapses to a persistent top-strip at 14pt.

#### 3. ★ The scrollbar IS the hockey stick
- **What:** No second chart. As the reader scrubs `p`, the browser's own horizontal scrollbar at the sheet's bottom shrinks in proportion. A 1px gold line drawn alongside the scrollbar rail plots test-R² at every width; a 1px dashed-gray line tracks `log λ_min(XᵀX)`. Past p=n both lines have fallen through the rail's floor; a small gold arrow points into the indigo margin labeled `R² = -3.4`. Implied y-axis tick-marks at the rail's left edge (`R²=1 | R²=0 | R²=−∞`). Caption: `# the scrollbar is the chart. the dashed line is why. you are holding the hockey stick.`
- **Why it works:** Answers the scrollytelling-format ESCALATE at the level of the browser chrome. The scroll is literally the feature-count axis; the chrome that carries the scroll is the chart. A paper cannot do this. A notebook cannot. A video cannot. Gestural lever: the chart exists only because the reader is performing the scroll gesture that makes it a chart.
- **Originated:** R6 Pedagogue "Scrollbar Is the Hockey Stick"; fused R6/R7 with Info Designer's eigenvalue sparkline as dashed overlay.
- **Implementation prerequisites:** Custom scrollbar styling must work across Safari, Firefox, Chrome, mobile. Mobile fallback: vertical edge-strip with the same affordance + column-counter chip. Dashed-gray for mechanism vs gold for symptom must be distinguishable at 1px.

## Chapter 4 — *Norway is a Scrabble score*

**Job of this chapter:** Deliver the full confession flat, uniform, in `print(model.coef_)` register — then refuse to narrate the verdict. The chapter is the site's one voiceless chapter. CH5 will keep score.

### Top wow-moments (ranked)

#### 1. ★★★ The β̂ row emits flat and uniform; the site refuses to score it
- **What:** No black viewport. No cursor. No 1.4s pacing. No brass tick. No 48pt Caslon. No role-color fill. Each cell contains four fixed-width columns per line: `+0.31 × name_numerology_score   [−0.14, +0.76]   194/200   +$24,100` (coefficient, bootstrap CI, stability fraction, per-Norway dollar contribution). Role-stroke underlines beneath each feature name are the reader's own CH1 tags, rendered at 40% opacity by default; on hover, they strengthen to 100%. One 9pt mono comment in the cream margin, the chapter's only annotation: `# print(model.coef_). compare the underlines to the brackets. ch5 will keep score.` **No DM Serif italic interjection.** CH4 is the site's one voiceless chapter. It looks exactly like `print(lasso.coef_.to_dict())` and it arrived in one frame. The reader who scrolls past reads nothing. Most will.
- **Why it works:** Devil's Advocate's R5 KILL in full. The pathos is exactly where DA placed it: in the reader's realization *they could have skipped it*. Aesthetic lever: the restraint is the teeth. The site's thesis — *no one reads a fit's coefficients carefully* — is teachable only if the emission feels skippable. The withheld verdict ("CH5 will keep score") is the format's pedagogical contract: the scroll captured the reader's priors in CH1, and CH4 is where the site refuses to congratulate them for being right.
- **Originated:** R1 Devil's Advocate AMPLIFY (confession as scar); refined across R2–R4 with typography-by-|β̂| and staging; *retired* R5 Devil's Advocate KILL of cinematic staging; committed as flat emission R6–R10.

No one proposes a closing serif line. The absent author-voice is the chapter's tightest constraint.

#### 2. ★★ The objection queue that runs dry at 8
- **What:** 9px `[×]` affordance at the right margin of every β̂ row line. Click: the line strikes through (120ms gold rule), the coefficient zeroes, **and Lasso substitutes the next feature from its waiting queue** — the substituted line appears at the bottom of the β̂ row in uniform type, same brackets, same stability count. Objections 1–3 replace with plausible features; objections 4–7 replace with progressively absurd ones (`+0.18 × un_member_letters`, `+0.15 × iso3_alphabetical_rank`); **objection 8 is silently refused**: `# objection 8 refused. there are no more features that produce this fit.` The reader has discovered, by their own gesture, that the confession is stable under perturbation: *every absurd feature they strike is replaced with another.*
- **Why it works:** The deepest lesson of the site: *there is no 20-feature Lasso fit on this data that doesn't lean on absurdity.* The reader discovers it as a gesture that fails, not a claim delivered. Gestural lever: the reader's agency hits a wall computed from the data itself. Narrative lever: the model doesn't need truth — it needs any 20 numbers.
- **Originated:** R2 Interaction Designer "Confession Booth"; refined R3 "Cross-Examined Confession"; sealed with `queue empty` refusal R7 Interaction Designer.
- **Implementation prerequisites:** Precomputed sequential-feature-selection refits for 8+ combinations, ordered by "next-best Lasso entry" — must be real math, not choreography. The `queue empty` message must be a real finding; if the 9th substitution is genuinely sensible in the analysis, the copy reports honestly ("the 9th replacement was `foreign_direct_investment_pct`, which is defensible — the queue is finite but not empty") and the chapter becomes quieter. Honesty outranks drama.
