## Round 5 · Cross-pollination · Devil's Advocate

### AMPLIFY
> Targeting Interaction Designer's **"The Column-Adding Gesture"** (CH2, Round 4)
> This is the first non-metaphorical rendering of overfitting in the entire transcript. A spreadsheet that **breaks under its own width** — cells overflowing into neighboring cells, digits spilling across column borders, scientific-notation tails colliding — is a disaster every analyst has experienced personally, and nobody has ever put it on a pedagogical site. Push it all the way: there is no hockey stick, no coefficient storm, no detaching piano keys, no "pins streaking off the rail." The crash *is* the CSV buckling. The reader drags the `p` cell; the β̂ row's values march into scientific notation, then into `Infinity`, then the cells physically exceed their borders and the layout engine stops holding. The chapter ends with a viewport full of `[object Object]` and overflow ellipses — the most honest screenshot of p > n ever produced. That is the 10× version. Pick it. Retire everything else.

### KILL
> Targeting **the Confession's cinematic staging** — 1.4s pacing, brass ticks, 48pt DM Serif, "full viewport black" (surviving in Aesthete's CH4 and Pedagogue's CH4)
> The committee agreed the Confession is the site's spine and it lives in the β̂ row of the spreadsheet. Good. Then why is it still being *staged* — black viewport, 1.4 seconds per line, sub-100ms brass ticks, 48pt Caslon? That is opera costume on a dataset's naked voice. A model's coefficients in a notebook don't arrive at 1.4s per line; they arrive in one frame, too fast to read, and you scroll back. Make the confession feel like `print(model.coef_)` — 18 lines appear at once in the β̂ row, monospace, 11pt, uniform weight, too fast. The reader has to *stop and read*. The struck-out lines from CH3 are grayed out in the same frame. There is no cursor animation. There is no tick. The model does not perform. The model **emits**. The pathos is in the reader's realization that they could have skipped over it — and many will. That is the scar.

### ESCALATE
> Round 5, and I still do not know **why this site is scrollytelling**. Every agent has assumed the scroll as the delivery format without defending it. The subject matter is a dataset, a CSV, a set of model fits — why isn't this a notebook, a paper, a video, a static gallery, a REPL? "Scrollytelling because Pudding" is the same answer as "brass because atlas." The committee is now working in dataset-native materiality for *content* but has not questioned the 2020s scrollytelling cliché as *format*. By Round 8 somebody must articulate, in one sentence, what the scroll specifically teaches that no other medium can. If the answer is "the structural confession accumulates across chapters," fine — defend it. If the answer is "we inherited the format," then the site is a Pudding clone in spreadsheet drag. Pick.
