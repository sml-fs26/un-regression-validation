## Round 7 · Cross-pollination · Interaction Designer

### CH1 — *Norway is indistinguishable from McDonald's*

- ★ **The Taxonomy Is Contestable From Keystroke One** *(amplifies DA R6 ESCALATE — "defend the labels or hand them over" + mashes Info Designer's role-lanes ECDF + Aesthete's role-color-as-stroke + my Suspect Board)*
  - **What:** The role-color is introduced not as authorial fiat but as a **gesture**. When the reader commits their CH1 double-click pick, the column header gets a 1px role-stroke — **and a small caret beside it**. Click the caret: the stroke cycles `spurious → causal → incidental → spurious`. Every chart in the site (the ECDF lanes, the null-band comparison, the CH4 β̂ row's role-colors, CH5's bracket-crosses-zero revelation) reconciles instantly. Beneath the caret, a 9pt mono line: `# taxonomy: 3 authorial / 353 unlabeled. click to contest.` The reader learns in CH1 that the color palette is a claim they can edit — and when they edit it, the site's argument has to re-render around their edit. The authors' confidence becomes a cell the reader owns.
  - **Why it works:** DA's ESCALATE in full. The site's thesis is epistemic humility; its palette was a confident hand; handing the palette to the reader makes the thesis operational. And it's the most committed form of direct manipulation — the reader doesn't just explore the data, they edit the ontology and watch the argument update. The site's own visual grammar bends to the reader's taxonomy.
  - **Risk / prerequisite:** Every role-color-dependent rendering must re-subscribe to a global taxonomy store; contested features' strokes must persist to CH5 or the gesture is theatre. At least three features must be genuinely ambiguous (`trade_openness` → causal or spurious? `population_density` → causal or incidental?) or the gesture exposes only edge cases.

- ★ **The Commit Pin Carries a Role the Reader Assigned** *(amplifies DA R4 AMPLIFY Norway-structural + DA R6 ESCALATE taxonomy-contestable + mashes Pedagogue's Norway-specific commit + Aesthete's pin-in-header)*
  - **What:** CH1's double-click commit still asks: "`column_A[NOR] = 9.4` or `column_B[NOR] = 14.0` — which would you stake Norway's wealth on?" But after the reveal, **the reader is asked to label the losing column**: a small three-way toggle appears beneath the unchosen card with `spurious / causal / incidental`. Whatever they pick becomes that column's sitewide role-stroke. The pin in Norway's row carries their label. In CH4, when the β̂ row emits, *the reader's own taxonomy is the one that colors the brackets* — the red strokes on `scrabble_letter_value` are there because the reader said so at minute one. If the reader labeled it `causal` at CH1, CH5's big reveal ("every red bracket crosses zero") reads differently for them.
  - **Why it works:** Taxonomy is not a thing done to the reader — it's a thing they *assigned* at commitment time. The CH5 payoff becomes not "the authors were right about red" but "your own labels agreed with the confidence intervals." Or, if they mislabeled, the site shows them their own incoherence without narrating it. The authorial fiat is dissolved at commit.
  - **Risk / prerequisite:** Requires a labeling UI that doesn't feel like a quiz — the three-way toggle must be one gesture, optional, with a sensible default (`spurious`) the reader can leave alone. Users who skip the labeling get the authorial taxonomy with a small `[authored]` badge beside each role-stroke in the site.

### CH2 — *Norway's prediction explodes*

- ★ **The Column That Tears Loose Can Be Pinned** *(amplifies DA R5 AMPLIFY spreadsheet-buckles-under-width + DA R6 AMPLIFY final-search-bar — makes p an explorable axis over named subsets + mashes my Feature Dial + Pedagogue's Budget of Surprise)*
  - **What:** As the CSV buckles and columns tear loose in eigenvalue-order, the reader can **pin a column to the model** by clicking any struck column-header before it leaves. Pinned columns resist the next tear; the β̂ row recomputes with the pin held. If the reader pins `rule_of_law_idx` early, it survives past p=n — and the reader watches its coefficient blow up anyway, proving the pin can't save it. If they pin `scrabble_letter_value`, they watch OLS amplify the absurd over the sensible. A sticky cell at top-right reads `pinned: 0 / p = 47` and updates live. The reader learns that keeping their favorites doesn't fix the matrix — the matrix fails regardless. Authorial preference is not a regularizer.
  - **Why it works:** Direct manipulation of the crash itself. The reader can *fight the crash* with their own priors and watch the crash win. This is the single best rebuttal to "well, I'd just keep the features I trust" — the site lets them try, and the CSV buckles under them holding their trusted features.
  - **Risk / prerequisite:** Pinning requires incremental refits; either precomputed small-feature-subset grids (up to 5 pins) or a fast client-side QR solve. The pin affordance must be discoverable (brief ghost suggestion: `click to keep`) but not pushy.

- ★ **Norway's Illegibility Is the Chapter's Signature Frame** *(amplifies DA R5 AMPLIFY "push the overflow all the way" + DA R4 Norway-biography + mashes my Norway's Row Screams + Aesthete's CSV-buckles + Pedagogue's Norway overflows)*
  - **What:** The CSV buckles per the round-6 commitment. The one committed refinement is this: **Norway's sticky row is the row that becomes illegible last**. Other countries' rows break first — their cells overflow earlier, their PI brackets render `Infinity` sooner. Norway holds, holds, holds — and at p=n−3 her `gdp_predicted` cell starts spilling into `pi_95%`, digits colliding, the `[−∞` of her interval crashing into the `+Infinity` of her prediction until the reader's eye cannot parse which number belongs to which cell. **The protagonist's row is the last typographic structure to fail, and it fails by merging with itself.** A 9pt mono line beneath: `Norway's values never stopped updating. you stopped being able to read them.` The TOC entry updates: `II. Norway ∈ [−∞, +∞].`
  - **Why it works:** DA's amplify wanted the overflow pushed all the way; DA's biographical spine wanted Norway to be the load-bearing typography. Both converge when the *last thing to break* is the reader's ability to read the protagonist's own row. The illegibility is the death — not a metaphor, not a chart, the reader's failed act of reading one row.
  - **Risk / prerequisite:** Tear-order must be such that Norway's row is last to overflow (verify in data — high-leverage points hold longest). The overflow merge between `gdp_predicted` and `pi_95%` must be deliberate — faint red-dashed broken-column boundary so readers don't misread it as bug.

### CH3 — *Norway is saved, wrongly*

- ★ **The Narrow PI Is Rendered IN the Color of What Narrowed It** *(direct answer to DA R6 KILL — CH3's signature in one pixel, not a footnote + mashes my Norway's Contribution Cell + Info Designer's per-country attribution + Aesthete's brass label retired)*
  - **What:** Norway's `pi_95%` cell, at CV-optimal α, reads `[$78,400, $94,900]`. **But the bracket itself is a micro-bar — a 60px horizontal bar inside the cell — divided into five segments whose widths are proportional to each surviving feature's contribution to the interval's narrowing, and whose colors are the role-strokes of those features.** At CV-optimal α the bar is: `[red 34% | red 28% | red 19% | red 11% | green 8%]`. **92% of the interval's narrowing is red.** The numeric bracket `[$78,400, $94,900]` floats above the bar in mono; the bar *is* the bracket, decomposed. The reader sees, in one pixel-region, the narrow PI (the triumph) and its absurd composition (the trap) — *same glance, same cell, no footnote, no hover.* Below the cell, one mono line: `Norway saved. 92% of the saving is absurd.`
  - **Why it works:** DA's KILL demanded the triumph and the trap in the same glance. Encoding the PI's *width* as a legitimate numeric (the bracket) while encoding its *color* as the role-stroke of the features that produced it puts both stories in one micro-bar. The reader cannot misread the chapter: the narrow interval is red-dominated, and the two facts cannot be separated because they're the same rectangle. CH3 earns its signature moment — a cell whose geometry is the triumph and whose color is the trap.
  - **Risk / prerequisite:** Per-feature contribution to interval narrowing must be a real computable quantity (marginal reduction in Var(ŷ|x_NOR) per feature, or a SHAP-for-variance proxy). The bar at 60px must stay legible; five segments is the cap. If any single spurious contribution dwarfs the others (one red segment at 80%), the chart is still honest and even more devastating.

- ★ **The α Cell Drags the Composition Bar** *(amplifies DA R6 KILL on CH3's signature + mashes my α Cell with 1-SE Annotation + Info Designer's 1-SE typographic state + Pedagogue's "gagging in two registers")*
  - **What:** The micro-bar inside Norway's PI cell is **live-linked to the α cell**. As the reader scrubs α, the bar re-proportions: at α=0, the bar is a 356-segment pandemonium (the buckled CH2 state, every feature contributing trace); at α=∞, the bar is empty (nothing narrows anything). Somewhere in between, the bar collapses to the 5-segment `[92% red | 8% green]` composition. **The reader watches the recovery of Norway's PI happen as the bar's colors shift — and sees that at every non-zero α, the composition is red-dominated.** There is no α where the bar turns mostly green. The "best α is a region" lesson from Info Designer's 1-SE annotation is in the same frame: the α cell renders gold within the 1-SE range, indigo outside — and across the entire gold range, Norway's bar is red. Triumph lives only in a red region.
  - **Why it works:** Makes CH3's single interaction (the α scrub) simultaneously drive CH3's signature pedagogical image (the triumph-is-the-trap bar). One gesture, one cell, one truth: *there is no α that saves Norway for honest reasons.* Answers DA's KILL and extends it — the reader doesn't just see the trap, they try to escape it and discover they can't.
  - **Risk / prerequisite:** Bar recomputation must be <100ms per α tick to feel continuous. The 1-SE gold/indigo typography on the α cell must be visually distinct from the micro-bar's colors (the cell styles the α value, the bar styles Norway's interval — different regions, no collision).

### CH4 — *Norway is a Scrabble score*

- ★ **The β̂ Row Is Objectable, But the Queue Runs Dry** *(amplifies DA R5 KILL emit-not-perform + DA R3 AMPLIFY structural confession + mashes my Cross-Examined Confession + Pedagogue's Norway's Equation)*
  - **What:** The β̂ row emits all 18 lines in one frame, uniform 11pt mono, per the committee's committed discipline. **Each line has a 9px `[×]` affordance at its right margin.** Click it: the line strikes through, the coefficient zeroes, **and Lasso substitutes the next feature from its waiting queue** — the substituted line appears at the bottom of the β̂ row in the same uniform type, same brackets, same stability count. The reader can object. The reader cannot out-object. Objections 1–3 replace with plausible-sounding features; objections 4–7 replace with progressively absurd ones (`+0.18 × un_member_letters`, `+0.15 × iso3_alphabetical_rank`); **objection 8 is silently refused — the queue is empty.** A single mono line beneath: `# objection 8 refused. there are no more features that produce this fit.` The reader has discovered, by their own gesture, that the model's confession is stable under perturbation: *every absurd feature the reader strikes is replaced with another absurdity.*
  - **Why it works:** DA's KILL wanted the confession to emit, not perform. This honors that — the lines appear at once, flat, boring. The drama is moved into the reader's objection gesture, which is itself dataset-native (`lasso.coef_[i] = 0; lasso.fit()`). The queue running dry is the deepest lesson of the site: *there is no 20-feature Lasso fit on this data that doesn't lean on absurdity.* The pedagogy lives in the gesture's failure.
  - **Risk / prerequisite:** Substitute-queue requires precomputed single-feature-knockout refits for at least 8 combinations, ordered by "next-best Lasso entry" — this must be real sequential-feature-selection, not choreography. The `queue empty` message must be a real finding; if the 9th substitution is genuinely sensible, the chapter reports it honestly and the closing line changes.

- ★ **The JOIN as the Chapter's Second Gesture** *(amplifies DA R4 ESCALATE dataset-as-material + DA R6 ESCALATE taxonomy-contestable + mashes my Two-Model JOIN + Info Designer's PDP-annotated columns + Aesthete's Dueling Spreadsheets)*
  - **What:** After the β̂ row's objection queue runs dry, a second sheet emits beneath: `rf_top_20.csv`. Reader drags any row from the Lasso β̂ row onto any row in the RF sheet; the drag draws a 1px gold hairline; the drop executes a relational join. The result renders as a third sheet: `feature | lasso_β | rf_importance | pdp_shape_agrees | reader_taxonomy | norway_contribution_usd`. **The `reader_taxonomy` column is populated by the role-strokes the reader assigned back in CH1.** If the reader labeled `scrabble_letter_value` as `spurious` at commit, that row's `reader_taxonomy` cell reads `spurious` and the JOIN's filter-by-taxonomy view lets them sort the agreement into their own categorical bins. The CH1 pin appears as a row where `rf_importance` is `NaN` and `reader_taxonomy` is whatever the reader called it — the most honest vindication (or self-indictment) SQL can render.
  - **Why it works:** Two gestures in CH4 (objection, JOIN) both answer DA's escalations — emit-not-perform and taxonomy-contestable. The JOIN result carries the reader's *own* categorical labels as a column, so the agreement analysis is run partly against their priors. If their labels were incoherent at CH1, the JOIN reveals the incoherence; if they were wise, the JOIN vindicates them. Either way, the authorial hand is not what's being judged.
  - **Risk / prerequisite:** `pdp_shape_agrees` column needs a rigorous definition (L1 distance on normalized PDPs, threshold at 0.15). `reader_taxonomy` column must default to authorial labels for features the reader didn't contest, with an explicit `[authored]` annotation.

### CH5 — *Norway was one draw*
