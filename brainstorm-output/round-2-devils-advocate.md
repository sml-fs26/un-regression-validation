## Round 2 · Divergence · Devil's Advocate

### AMPLIFY
> Targeting Aesthete's "The Coefficient Storm" (CH2)
> This is the strongest non-Cartesian image in the entire Round 1 haul — pins that vibrate, detach, and streak off the axis like long-exposure rain. But you've framed it as decoration on top of the hockey stick. Make it **replace** the hockey stick. The chapter opens with a hundred pins at rest. The reader drags `p`. Past `p=n`, pins tear loose, streak off-screen, and leave scars on the axis — and *those scars* are what accumulates into the R² curve, drawn as a negative-space ghost. The crash is not a line going down; the crash is **debris**. If CH2 still has a hockey stick after this, you haven't earned the storm.

### KILL
> Targeting Information Designer's "Boxplot with strip plot overlay" (CH5)
> A boxplot of 200 splits with a circled dot labeled "the number we reported." I have seen this exact chart in every replication-crisis explainer since 2015. It is correct, it is honest, and it is invisible. CH5 is the **trust** chapter — the whole thesis lands here — and its signature image is a boxplot. Either CH5 gets a moment that doesn't exist in any stats textbook, or admit the chapter is a footnote and cut it to two screens. Candidate brief for what belongs here: an image where the reader *watches a single headline number dissolve* into the cloud that always surrounded it. Not a boxplot with an annotation. A dissolution.

### ESCALATE

Feature dial, α slider, kill-a-feature game, shuffle button, scrubber of regret, alpha tuning fork, shrinkage dial, slot machine, three-up α sweep. Nine. We agreed the rule is one slider per scene, and Interaction Designer's instinct to make every chapter draggable has infected the whole outline. Pick the **two** interactions in the entire site that matter most and kill the other seven. A site where every chapter hands the reader a knob is a site where no chapter commits to an argument. Which two survive? Defend them by round 4.
