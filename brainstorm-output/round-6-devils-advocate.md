### ESCALATE
> The color grammar — green = causal, red = spurious, gray = incidental — has been the site's primary visual vocabulary since Round 1. Nobody has asked: **who labeled the features?** The authors did. The entire site is an argument that letting humans name truth from data is how you end up with `name_numerology_score` predicting national wealth — yet the color system presupposes the authors *already knew* which features were truth, lies, and noise. This is the one piece of authorial fiat the committee has never examined. The site is teaching epistemic humility in a palette written by a confident hand. Either (a) derive the taxonomy on camera — show why `rule_of_law_idx` is green and `mcdonalds_per_million` is red, with the evidence typeset in the site's own grammar — or (b) let the reader contest it. Click `mcdonalds_per_million`, change its stroke from red to gray, and every chart in the site reconciles. If neither happens, the site's graphic design contradicts its thesis. Defend the labels, or hand them over.
