## Round 8 · Synthesis · Interaction Designer

### CH1 — *Norway is indistinguishable from McDonald's*

- ★ **The Entrance: Filename, Ticker, Tag, Commit, Whisper**
  - **What:** The site opens *inside* the sheet — no two-page spread, no page-fold, no 240pt Caslon. Top-left, 11pt mono: `# five_deaths_of_norway.ipynb`. The sheet is already visible; Norway's row is already gold. A second sticky cell beside her ISO-3: `h_ii = 0.04`, ticking. The reader's first 90 seconds are a single braided gesture: (1) type three columns into the query bar — the ECDF tick-lane assembles in their peripheral vision until a mono line reads `5/5 of your guesses beat chance`; (2) commit on two anonymized columns — double-click the one they'd stake Norway's wealth on — the unchosen column gets a ◌ pin in its header; (3) on the reveal, a three-way toggle offers `[causal] [spurious] [incidental]` for each column, and the reader *tags* — not the authors — the first 10 features with 1px strokes that now render sitewide; (4) one second later, the first β̂ row cell whispers, specifically about the column they pinned: `> β̂[scrabble_letter_value_of_capital_name] = +0.71 [95% CI: +0.63, +0.78]`. Five reader-gestures, one mono header, one ticker, one pin, and one whispered coefficient that is about *their* specific misjudgment. No title card is rendered. No ceremony is performed. The CSV is the publisher; the filename is the title.
  - **Why it works:** Answers Devil's R7 KILL (filename-not-Caslon), R7 AMPLIFY (`h_ii` as ticker), R6 ESCALATE (reader-authored taxonomy), R4 AMPLIFY (Norway structural from keystroke one), and R3 AMPLIFY (structural confession begins as whisper about the reader's pick). Five escalations, one entrance, one minute thirty. The reader has been the analyst, the taxonomist, and the confession's first audience before they know any of those words apply.
  - **Risk / prerequisite:** Every gesture here is irrevocable within the session — no undo on tags, no re-pick on the commit. The `h_ii` ticker must be visually calm (9pt mono, gray gutter) or it competes. The whisper's pin-targeting requires two-branch copy (right pick / wrong pick).

### CH2 — *Norway's prediction explodes*

- ★ **The Crash Is the Interface, the Ticker Is the Biography**
  - **What:** The reader drags the `p` cell. Three things buckle simultaneously — and they are the **same** buckling: (1) **the spreadsheet** — columns append rightward, β̂ values march `0.31 → 847.3 → +2.3e4 → +Infinity`, cells overflow their borders with dashed-red broken-boundary hairlines, Norway's `pi_95%` renders `[−∞, +∞]`; (2) **the browser's own scrollbar** — a 1px gold test-R² line plots alongside its rail, falls through the rail's floor past p=n, and a small gold arrow points into the indigo margin labeled `R² = -3.4`; (3) **the ticker** — `h_ii = 0.04 → 0.41 → 0.83 → 0.98`, climbing beside Norway's ISO-3 in the same gutter it has lived in since CH1. The reader discovers a single affordance: click any column header before it tears loose to **pin** it to the model. They fight the crash with their priors — pin `rule_of_law_idx`, pin `mcdonalds_per_million`, whatever — and watch OLS blow up the pinned coefficients *anyway*. The β̂ row accumulates confession lines into its expanding height: `+847.3 × scrabble_letter_value_of_capital_name`, the model's words *crowding past its own column borders* as the sheet fails. At p=n−3, Norway's row is the **last typographic structure to break**, and it breaks by merging with itself — her `gdp_predicted` digits collide with her `pi_95%` cell's `[−∞`, and the reader's eye cannot parse which number belongs to which cell. One mono line below: `Norway's values never stopped updating. you stopped being able to read them.` TOC updates: `II. Norway ∈ [−∞, +∞].`
  - **Why it works:** Devil's R5 AMPLIFY (push the CSV overflow all the way), R7 AMPLIFY (`h_ii` as the structural number), R4 ESCALATE (dataset as material at the browser chrome), and R5 ESCALATE (the scroll *is* the feature-count axis, defended) all converge in one frame. The reader's hand is on the scroll-handle of the hockey stick; the ticker is the mechanism; the sheet's failure to hold Norway is the death; the pin gesture is the reader's prior bent and broken. Four commitments, one scroll.
  - **Risk / prerequisite:** Tear-order must genuinely place Norway last (leverage does this, but verify). Scrollbar styling cross-browser; mobile replaces with a vertical column-counter and the gold line becomes an edge-strip. Pinning requires incremental refit for up to 5 pins — precomputed knockout grid or fast client-side QR.

### CH3 — *Norway is saved, wrongly*

- ★ **The Cell That Contains Its Own Refutation, Live-Linked to α**
  - **What:** Devil's R6 KILL is paid in full and I will not share the credit: CH3 has exactly **one signature cell**. Norway's `pi_95%` cell, at CV-optimal α, renders two lines of text inside a single cell border: line 1 is the gold bracket `[$78,400, $94,900]`; line 2, in 7pt mono directly beneath, is `= 0.34·num + 0.28·mcd + 0.19·scr + 0.11·b/w + 0.08·rule`. The five abbreviations carry 1px underline-strokes in the reader's own CH1-assigned role-colors — four red, one green. **The triumph (narrow bracket) and the trap (absurd decomposition) share a cell border and cannot be separated by any glance.** The reader's α-cell scrub (the chapter's only live control) **drives both lines of the cell simultaneously** — at α=0 the bracket reads `[−∞, +∞]` and line 2 is a 356-term pandemonium; at α=∞ the cell is empty; at CV-optimal α it collapses to `[$78k, $94k] = [92% red terms]`. Across every α where the reader's α-cell renders gold (inside 1-SE), Norway's cell is red-dominated. There is no α where the bar turns mostly green. Beside it, the shuffled-twin cell `pi_95%_shuffled` renders identically narrow and identically red. Above Norway's row, the ticker: `h_ii_Norway = 0.31` — recovered, stable, and still 98% her own prediction. Strikethroughs on 336 struck column headers inherit the reader's CH1 tags: columns the reader labeled `causal` that Lasso zeros out get struck in **green** (visually loud); columns labeled `spurious` that Lasso *keeps* get a thin red persistence-box — a live audit of the reader's taxonomy against the regularizer's priorities. Counter above the sheet: `your labels vs Lasso's picks: agree on 14 | disagree on 7`. No museum label. No footnote. The chapter's brass allowance is spent on *nothing*; the cell is the citation.
  - **Why it works:** Devil's R6 KILL (triumph as evidence of the trap), R6 ESCALATE (reader-authored palette doing pedagogical work), R4 ESCALATE (dataset-native grammar), and R7 AMPLIFY (leverage ticker) close in one cell. The reader cannot scroll past CH3 having seen only the narrowing; the cell's second line is inside the cell's first. The α-scrub lets them hunt for a green α and discover no such α exists. Three chapters of tags and pins and pegs pay off as live audit. CH3 stops being a bridge.
  - **Risk / prerequisite:** Two-line cell rendering at Norway's column width is tight; 7pt mono with full names on hover. Per-feature contribution-to-variance-narrowing must be a real quantity (marginal reduction in Var(ŷ|x_NOR)). If one spurious contribution dwarfs the others, the bar is even more devastating. The role-color-as-fill on line 2's underlines is the *one* exception to the stroke-only rule sitewide — and it earns the exception.

### CH4 — *Norway is a Scrabble score*

- ★ **The Emission, the Objection Queue That Runs Dry, the JOIN That Renders the Reader's Priors as a Column**
  - **What:** No black viewport. No 1.4s pacing. No brass tick. The β̂ row **expands vertically in one frame** to render 18 Lasso coefficients, uniform 11pt JetBrains Mono Medium, no hierarchy, four fixed-width columns per line: `coefficient | CI_bracket | stability_fraction | norway_contribution_usd`. Example: `+0.31 × name_numerology_score   [−0.14, +0.76]   194/200   +$24,100`. Eighteen lines. Same weight. Same color. **Role-color is hover-revealed only** — 1px underline-strokes fade up beneath each feature name on dwell, and the strokes are *the reader's own CH1 tags*. A small 9pt mono comment in the cream margin: `# print(model.coef_)`. That comment is the chapter's only annotation. Each line has a 9px `[×]` affordance; click it, the coefficient zeroes, and Lasso **substitutes the next feature from its queue** — a substituted line appears at the bottom of the β̂ row in the same uniform type. Objections 1–3 replace with plausible features; objections 4–7 substitute progressively absurd ones; **objection 8 is silently refused**: `# objection 8 refused. there are no more features that produce this fit.` The reader has discovered, with their own hand, that the confession is stable under perturbation — *every absurdity they strike is replaced with another.* Beneath, a mono line types itself in-frame: `result = pd.merge(lasso_top20, rf_top20, on='feature', how='outer')`. The JOIN executes; a sheet renders with six columns: `feature | lasso_β | rf_importance | pdp_shape_agrees | reader_tag | norway_contrib`. **The `reader_tag` column is populated by the reader's CH1 labels.** Of 6 rows where both models agree, 5 have `reader_tag = causal` (the reader's own taxonomy tracks cross-model agreement). Norway's per-feature contribution sums: `agreed features → 8% of predicted log-GDP; Lasso-only features → 75%`. The CH1 pin appears as a JOINed row: `{lasso_β: +0.27, rf_importance: NaN, pdp_shape_agrees: null, reader_tag: spurious, norway_contrib: +$19,600}`. The reader's original skepticism returns as a row in their own grammar. No "you knew better in 8 seconds" serif line — the JOIN row *is* the serif line.
  - **Why it works:** Devil's R5 KILL (emit not perform), R3 AMPLIFY (structural confession reaches full form), R4 ESCALATE (dataset grammar for model comparison), and R6 ESCALATE (reader's taxonomy doing the work) all close in the chapter the committee spent most rounds agonizing over. The emission is uniform; the pathos is in the reader's realization that they could have skipped it; the objection queue running dry is the deepest lesson in the site (*there is no 20-feature fit on this data that does not lean on absurdity*); the JOIN result renders the reader's priors as a queryable column. Four rounds of DA collapse into one chapter that performs its indictment without staging.
  - **Risk / prerequisite:** Substitution queue requires precomputed single-feature-knockout refits for 8 combinations, ordered by real sequential-feature-selection — no choreography. The `pd.merge` one-liner must actually execute a relational join in <200ms; it's not a rendered literal. `reader_tag` defaults to `untagged` for features the reader didn't label. The 8% / 75% split must be a real computed ratio. Hover-reveal on strokes must be touch-friendly (tap-to-dwell).

### CH5 — *Norway was one draw*

- ★ **The Dissolution, the Bracketed Verdict, the Recast Bar**
  - **What:** One scene in three acts, all in uniform 11pt mono and one 180pt numeral (the single allowed DM Serif moment in the chapter). **Act one:** `Norway.predicted_gdp = $89,220` in 180pt gold, held 2 seconds; dissolves into 200 per-split predictions for Norway specifically; the numeral's pixels recruit into ghost dots. The reader's familiar value becomes one faintly gold-tinted dot in a cloud spanning `−$8k to +$380k`. The cloud splits vertically into two ridgelines on shared axes — top: single-split distribution, bottom: honest nested-CV. A vertical gold hairline between the means: `optimism gap: $51,800 per Norwegian, per year the model ran`. Norway's sticky row: `pi_95% across 200 splits = [$12k, $2.2M]`. The ticker: `h_ii_Norway_bootstrap_mean = 0.29 ± 0.14`. **Act two:** the mono paragraph of 200 Norways is **sortable**; one click sorts the reader's reported `$89,220` to its true percentile — it was in the top quintile, the 79th percentile. `our draw was in the good 20% of possible worlds` types below. **Act three:** the β̂ row returns one final time — uniform 11pt mono, flat emission, same as CH4 — but now every coefficient carries a hairline-gold 95% bootstrap CI bracket, and **the role-color strokes are revealed-by-default for the first time in the site** because *they agree exactly with CI behavior.* Every red-tagged feature: bracket crosses zero. The green-tagged feature: bracket holds. The reader's own CH1 taxonomy is typeset against bootstrap calibration. One right-aligned mono line: `your labels agreed with the brackets N times out of 18. the math does not care which of you was right.` Then, beneath it — the closing image — a 32pt DM Serif italic plate: `every country in this dataset has five deaths. we only told you about one.` And beneath *that*, one 11pt mono input cell: `recast: ___`. Autocomplete on ISO-3. Type `URY`. **The site re-performs.** The filename at top-left rewrites: `# five_deaths_of_uruguay.ipynb`. The TOC re-titles. The buckled CH2 re-buckles around Uruguay's PI. The CH3 narrow-PI cell re-typesets with her specific composition. The CH4 β̂ row re-emits with her attributions. The ticker reads `h_ii_Uruguay = ...`. Five chapters. One template. Performed on demand for whoever the reader names.
  - **Why it works:** Devil's R4 KILL (no rose window), R5 KILL (no cinematic staging), R3 AMPLIFY (CIs wider than coefficients), R6 AMPLIFY (search bar makes the aphorism executable), and R6 ESCALATE (reader-taxonomy verdict) all land in one chapter. The site's ending is not rhetorical; it is a specification the site can execute. The scroll was the tutorial; the search bar is the deployed analysis. The reader who scrolled through Norway now reads Uruguay's five deaths in the grammar they just learned. The aphorism is no longer a flourish — it is the README for the instrument beneath it.
  - **Risk / prerequisite:** Nested CV must be really run. The 79th-percentile claim must be real; if the reported value was median, copy honestly says so. Recast requires precomputed arcs for all 254 countries (or at least top 30 audition winners with loading state for the rest). **Failure mode**: when a country's arc is muted (CH3 trap is quieter than Norway's), the site renders honest empty cells with a mono line: `Uruguay's trap at chapter III: muted. The model never lied loudly about her. Five deaths require a country the model is willing to kill.` Honesty as feature.

### X-CUT — Cross-cutting

- ★ **The Ticker, the Tag-Column, the Filename, the Recast Bar: the site's architecture resolves into four elements**
  - **What:** Synthesis across seven rounds of critique, answered at the artifact level. **(1) The filename** at top-left, 11pt mono — the title is `# five_deaths_of_norway.ipynb` and becomes `# five_deaths_of_uruguay.ipynb` when the reader recasts. No book cover; the publisher is the dataset. **(2) The `h_ii` ticker** beside the protagonist's ISO-3, always visible across five chapters, 9pt mono in the gutter, updating live as `p`, `α`, and bootstrap states change — the single structural scalar whose five-chapter arc is the biography Devil's Advocate found the committee had been missing. **(3) The `role` column** pinned at the leftmost position of the CSV, editable at any time, populated by the reader's CH1 tags and any subsequent overrides; a small counter reads `labeled: 42/356 (authored: 0, by you: 42). 314 unlabeled.` Every chart in the site subscribes to this column and reconciles in <300ms when the reader edits it. The authorial hand is gone; the 314-unlabeled disclosure is the admission the site's palette is the reader's. **(4) The recast bar** as the closing interaction — the site's final gesture is its deployment. Scroll to learn the grammar; recast to apply it; tag-column to contest it. Three operations, four elements. Everything else — the `p` cell, the `α` cell, the `[×]` objections, the double-click commit, the sortable Dissolution, the hover-reveal strokes — is a **cell edit**. Two live controls, one taxonomy-column, one input, one ticker, one filename. The site's architecture is legible in a single screenshot at any chapter boundary.
  - **Why it works:** Every Devil's Advocate escalation across rounds 3 through 7 resolves here — nine sliders become two cells and one input; brass instruments become zero (one museum-label allowance goes unused in the final consolidation); the cemetery becomes the ticker; the rose window becomes typed ISO-3; the authorial color becomes the reader's column; the scrollytelling format becomes tutorial-plus-deployment. The site's argument — *no one reads a fit's coefficients carefully* — is taught by making the reader read one carefully, and then handing them the instrument to read 253 more. The closing line is no longer an aphorism; it is an affordance the site has proven it can honor.
  - **Risk / prerequisite:** Committee commitment across all five chapters to this four-element discipline, policed in code review. Every element must have session-persistent state. The recast bar's compute budget is the single largest engineering ask; the fallback (precompute top 30, on-demand compute for the rest with a 4-second mono loading state `computing five deaths for MNG …`) is acceptable but not deniable. If the recast doesn't work, the closing line is a bluff and the site must retreat — there is no middle ground.

- ★ **Why the Scroll, Answered Three Ways, Closed Once**
  - **What:** The scroll is defended three times by the three agents who co-own the site, and the three defenses compose into one. **Aesthete:** the scroll uniquely enables peripheral perception — evidence accumulates in the margin for 25 minutes until the reader realizes the confession they could have read in 10 seconds was always bracketed. **Pedagogue:** the scroll is the only medium in which the reader's *early* gestures become *later* evidence, with no opportunity to revise; the CH1 tag becomes CH5 verdict with no retroactive correction, which is the reader-side analog of the statistical pitfall the site teaches. **Information Designer:** the scroll's failure mode *is* the pedagogy — the reader who scrolls past `print(model.coef_)` in two seconds has just enacted the exact crime the site is teaching, and no other medium instruments that failure. All three are true, all three are required. The composite defense: **the scroll is the only format that (a) gate-keeps attention linearly, (b) captures the reader's priors before the evidence arrives, and (c) punishes inattention by the same mechanism it rewards attention.** And then the scroll ends — and the recast bar begins. The scroll was the tutorial; the bar is the tool. The scroll earns its format by producing a reader who can use the bar. Everything else in the site is the justification for this handoff.
  - **Why it works:** Devil's R5 ESCALATE asked the committee to defend the format in one sentence. The committee now has three defenses, each true, and one handoff that makes all three matter. The scroll is not inherited; the scroll is the onboarding for the analysis beneath it. A page the reader reads is a page that trained a reader. No other medium does this. If the site's scroll and recast bar both exist and both work, the format question is settled.
  - **Risk / prerequisite:** Every chapter after CH1 must visibly reference at least one reader-authored state from an earlier chapter — otherwise the peripheral-capture is undetectable and the defense is rhetoric. Code-review rule: no chapter works as a standalone gallery image; each requires prior-chapter state. Build-time check: a reader who lands directly on `/iv-scrabble-score` must see "to read this chapter, scroll from the top" — not a helpful tooltip but a refusal. The scroll must be required, or it is not defended.
