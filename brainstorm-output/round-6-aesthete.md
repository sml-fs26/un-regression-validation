### X-CUT — Cross-cutting

- ★ **The Defense of Scroll — Answer to Devil's Advocate's Round 5 ESCALATE** *(amplify: Devil's R5 ESCALATE on format justification + mashup: the structural-confession spine + the accumulating-uncertainty marginalia + the biographical-protagonist arc)*
  - **What:** The scroll is not the format because Pudding. The scroll is the format for one specific reason, which I will commit to defending in one sentence: **the site teaches that uncertainty is what you failed to notice, and only the scroll allows evidence to accumulate in the reader's peripheral vision for 25 minutes until they realize the confession they could have read in 10 seconds was always bracketed.** A notebook is self-paced but requires running cells; a video dictates pace; a static gallery shows everything at once; a REPL rewards prior statistical literacy. The scroll uniquely enables a structure where (a) the model's voice whispers in CH1 and the reader does not yet know it is a voice, (b) the β̂ row accumulates across chapters such that CH4's emission is felt as familiar rather than new, (c) the bracket grammar that will undo the confession in CH5 has been rendered in the marginalia since CH1 without the reader's knowledge. The site's pedagogy *is* peripheral perception; the scroll is peripheral perception's only medium. If this site were a notebook, the β̂ row would not accumulate; the confession would be a single cell's output; the uncertainty would be a whisker in a plot. It would teach none of what this site teaches.
  - **Why it works:** Devil's Advocate's ESCALATE is the sharpest provocation of the round and I will not dodge it. The defense must be specific, and the specificity is: *the scroll is the only medium in which the reader is responsible for the pace but is not responsible for noticing what accumulates.* Every other medium either shows you everything (gallery), plays at its own pace (video), requires your action to advance (notebook), or presumes you already know what to look for (REPL). The scroll reads you while you read it.
  - **Risk / prerequisite:** The committee must commit to peripheral-perception pedagogy in every chapter — if any chapter front-loads its confession or surfaces its brackets without requiring the reader to have scrolled past them once, the defense is false and the format is indefensible. Code review rule: every chapter must carry at least one piece of evidence that is legible only by the reader who has read the prior chapters.
