### CH2 — Norway's Prediction Explodes

The CH4 emit-don't-perform beat (DA's KILL) *requires* the four chapters of prior whispering to make the reader realize they almost skipped over the payoff. The scroll is not "because Pudding." The scroll is because the site's thesis is *no one reads a fit's coefficients carefully*, and the only way to prove that to a reader is to let them almost not read ours.
  - **Why it works:** Every other thread in the site collapses into this defense. The β̂ row's persistence across chapters requires sequence. The protagonist's five deaths require order. The CH1 commitment requires futurity. The Dissolution requires the headline to have been trusted. Each is a scrollytelling-native pedagogy — not a scrollytelling decoration.   - **Risk / prerequisite:** The defense only holds if every chapter genuinely uses sequence as a pedagogical tool — no chapter can be a standalone scene.
