### CH1 — *Norway is indistinguishable from McDonald's*

- ★ **The Query Bar That Writes the ECDF** *(amplifies DA R4 AMPLIFY Norway-structural + DA R4 ESCALATE dataset-as-material + mashes my Norway Search Bar + Pedagogue's "Scrolling CSV With One Row Highlighted" + Info Designer's ECDF-by-role + Aesthete's typeset CSV)*
  - **What:** The site opens on the typeset CSV — `NOR` sticky-gold at top — and a single input above it: `predict(Norway.gdp) ≈ corr(gdp, _____)`. Autocomplete from the 356 real column names. The reader types three columns (or clicks three headers); each query drops a tick on a small ECDF strip above the bar — green/red/gray lanes form under the reader's fingers. After their third query — *any three* — a quiet mono line appears beneath the strip: `you just drew the null band. 186 / 355 features would have landed where yours did.` The reader hasn't looked at a chart; they've **produced the pitfall** in three keystrokes.
  - **Why it works:** DA ESCALATE (format): the scroll-interaction isn't a scroll — it's a REPL. The reader is doing correlation-fishing in real time, in the dataset's own typography, and the ECDF assembles itself as a byproduct. No "discover that correlation is weak"; the reader runs the experiment. Norway is the query target from keystroke one, which is DA's AMPLIFY made gestural.
  - **Risk / prerequisite:** Autocomplete must be fast; first-query ghost text (`try "rule_of_law_idx"`) must be unobtrusive; mobile replaces autocomplete with a tappable roster of 20 pre-surfaced columns.

- ★ **The Whisper Emits, Doesn't Perform** *(amplifies DA R5 KILL — emit not perform + mashes Aesthete's first whisper + Pedagogue's overheard coefficient + Info Designer's bracketed first line)*
  - **What:** DA's KILL applied in CH1. After the commit-by-double-click, **no typing animation, no cursor theatre**. One line appears in the β̂ row of the CSV, all at once, in JetBrains Mono 11pt, uniform weight, same color as every other cell: `coef_[scrabble_letter_value_of_capital] = +0.71   [95%: +0.63, +0.78]`. It's not announced. It's not spotlit. It's a cell value. The reader who scrolls past it misses it; the reader who stops and reads it has done the work. The model isn't whispering — the model is **already there**, and the reader is learning to notice.
  - **Why it works:** DA is right — 1.4s pacing is opera costume. `print(model.coef_)` appears in one frame. Making the confession emit instead of perform means the reader's attention is the pedagogy, not the animation. Sets the grammar for every subsequent chapter: the β̂ row **publishes**, the reader **reads**.
  - **Risk / prerequisite:** Must resist every collaborator's urge to add typewriter pacing, cursor blinks, ticks, or gold halos. If the line pulses once on insert, that's the single allowed affordance — it tells the reader something updated.

### CH2 — *Norway's prediction explodes*

- ★ **The Spreadsheet Buckles Under Its Own Width** *(amplifies DA R5 AMPLIFY — push the CSV-overflow gesture all the way + mashes my Column-Adding Gesture + Info Designer's Detaching Column + Aesthete's Ledger Spreadsheet + Pedagogue's Budget of Surprise + my Feature Dial)*
  - **What:** **There is no hockey stick. No pins. No piano keys. No coefficient storm. The crash is the layout engine giving up.** The reader scrubs the `p` cell. As columns append, the β̂ row's values march through scale: `0.31` → `847.3` → `2.1e4` → `3.7e18` → `Infinity`. Cells stop holding their borders — digits spill rightward into neighboring headers, scientific-notation tails collide, Norway's `PI_95%` cell renders literally `[-Infinity, +Infinity]`. At p > n the β̂ row visibly overflows its bounds; the horizontal scrollbar thins to a thread; a few cells render `NaN` and a few render `[object Object]`. The reader's final screenshot is a CSV **in the visual state every analyst has seen in their own notebook at 2am** — a sheet that tried to hold more columns than it had rows. No chart frames. Just the sheet, broken. One caption in 9pt mono beneath the ruin: `# this is what p > n looks like before anyone draws it.`
  - **Why it works:** DA's AMPLIFY paid in full. This is the first image in the site's entire genre that isn't a stylization of failure — it's **the actual failure state of the interface**. Overfitting is not a hockey stick; overfitting is a spreadsheet that outgrew its data and broke. Answers DA's ESCALATE about format in one gesture: scrollytelling here earns its scroll because you physically drag the sheet wider than the screen can hold; a static image can't show you that. The scroll IS the mechanism.
  - **Risk / prerequisite:** Overflow must be *deliberate* — a subtle persistent red-dashed hairline on the ruined cells prevents "this looks like a bug" misreads. `Infinity` and `NaN` are real OLS outputs past p=n and must not be softened. Reduced-motion shows three pinned snapshots (p=50, p=n, p=356) with the final one rendered as a static broken-CSV PNG.

- ★ **Norway's Row Is the One You Can't Read Anymore** *(amplifies DA R4 AMPLIFY Norway-biography + mashes Aesthete's "Norway's Row Speaks" + Pedagogue's "Norway's Row Overflows" + Info Designer's per-country PI + my "Throw Norway Into the Test Set")*
  - **What:** Through the buckling, **Norway's sticky row is the one cell cluster the reader keeps watching**. Three cells: `gdp_actual = 89154`, `gdp_predicted`, `pi_95%`. At p=50 the cells are clean. At p=200 `gdp_predicted` is still 89,219 but `pi_95%` reads `[12200, 380000]` and the cell's width is visibly straining. At p=n the row *itself* has become illegible: `gdp_predicted` contains `3.71e17`, `pi_95%` contains `[-Infinity, Infinity]` spilling past its border into `gdp_per_capita`, and `NOR` in the gutter is the only thing still set in legible gold. The biographical line at the bottom reads: `Norway is still here. you can no longer read her row.`
  - **Why it works:** Norway as structural protagonist means her *row* is the load-bearing typography. When the spreadsheet breaks, her row breaking is Death II — not metaphorically, typographically. The reader watches the protagonist's cells overflow into adjacent columns; the visual grammar of "prediction is worse than useless" is *illegibility in her own row*. No ribbon, no envelope, no scream — the cells themselves become unreadable.
  - **Risk / prerequisite:** The pathological Norway numbers must be real (verify in the analysis — `3.71e17` should be genuinely what OLS returns for Norway at p=356 under some reasonable parametrization). The illegibility must be deliberate, not accidental overflow. Mobile: Norway's row collapses to a persistent top banner that itself breaks; the effect must survive.

### CH3 — *Norway is saved, wrongly*

  - **What:** The buckled CSV stays on screen, still ruined. The reader is given one rescue tool: a single editable cell `α = 0.00`. Click-drag to scrub. As α rises, three things happen **in the same frame, without animation**: (a) column headers whose Lasso coefficient crossed zero render as `~~strikethrough~~` in gold, (b) the β̂ row values shrink back from `Infinity` into readable scale, cells re-seating within their borders, (c) a pinned 45°-striped column at the far left — `gdp_shuffled` — updates its twin quantities: `CV_R² = 0.48` beside `CV_R²_shuffled = 0.11`, `nonzero = 21` beside `nonzero_shuffled = 18`. The reader fixes the CSV with α; the reader also sees the shuffled column do almost the same thing. Norway's row becomes legible again — `pi_95% = [78k, 102k]`, triumph — **and right beside it, in the shuffled twin, Norway's shuffled `pi_95% = [11k, 1.2M]` is also narrower than it was.** The rescue is real. The rescue is also available on pure noise.
  - **Why it works:** DA's ESCALATE on dataset-native: the null is a column. The false dawn is now an observable in the spreadsheet, not a scenic cue. And per DA's KILL — nothing performs. The α cell publishes a new state; the reader reads what changed. The CSV stops being broken; the CSV's shuffled twin also stops being broken. The reader seeds their own CH4 doubt.
  - **Risk / prerequisite:** Shuffled column must stay pinned regardless of horizontal scroll; hiding it is dishonest. Click-drag-to-scrub needs a discoverable affordance on numeric cells — subtle cursor change + a one-time `drag to change α` microcopy on hover.

- ★ **Norway's Contribution Cell Is a Sparkline of Absurdities** *(amplifies DA R4 Norway-structural + mashes my Entry-Order Racetrack + Info Designer's per-country PDP + Pedagogue's Podium Plate + Aesthete's Museum Label)*

  - **Why it works:** Biographical spine lives in one cell. ### X-CUT — Cross-cutting

- ★ **Why the Scroll: Because the Scroll Is the Column Count** *(defends DA R5 ESCALATE on the format + mashes my every-interaction-is-a-cell-edit + Aesthete's horizontal CSV scroll + Info Designer's "Sheet / Row / Voice one object")*
  - **What:** **Formal defense of scrollytelling, in one sentence: the reader's scroll direction IS the feature count axis.** CH1 begins at column 1 of the CSV; as the reader scrolls, they physically travel through the 356 columns, and the *horizontal extent of the page* is the site's y-axis on every chart that would otherwise need one. CH2's spreadsheet-buckling gesture is not a metaphor — the reader is scrolling the sheet wider and **watching the layout engine fail**, which no notebook, no static plot, no paper, no video can show; a REPL could but wouldn't accumulate the protagonist's running diagnostic cells across chapters. The scroll is not inherited from Pudding; the scroll is recruited because **the subject of the site is a horizontally-overlong dataset and the reader's scroll is a feature-index slider**. By CH5 the reader has scrolled through 356 columns of one CSV — which is the dataset — and read every chapter's signature image as a transform of cells in the sheet they've been scrolling. The scroll earns its place. Everything else is a cell edit.
  - **Why it works:** DA's ESCALATE deserved a defense and now has one. The format is justified by being co-extensive with the dataset's actual shape. The scroll gate-keeps attention (which DA's KILL demanded — no performance, the reader must stop and read); the scroll is also the sheet's horizontal travel (which DA's ESCALATE on dataset-as-material demanded). Two critiques answered with one structural commitment. If we couldn't defend this, we'd refactor to a notebook; we can, so we don't.
  - **Risk / prerequisite:** The site's horizontal-scroll-as-p-axis must be visually legible (a thin column-counter chip at the bottom always reads `column 47 of 356`). Mobile: the horizontal scroll becomes a virtualized horizontal swipe within a pinned viewport, and the column-counter becomes the primary wayfinding device. If this feels awkward on touch, the format argument weakens — so prototype early.

- ★ **Three Voices, One Emission Grammar** *(amplifies DA R5 KILL emit-not-perform + DA R4 ESCALATE + DA R3 AMPLIFY structural confession + mashes Pedagogue's Three Voices + Aesthete's Atlas Is a Spreadsheet + my Every Interaction Is a Cell Edit)*
  - **What:** Committee-level rule, sitewide: **nothing in the site types, cursors, ticks, fades, or staggers. Everything emits.** The data emits in 11pt JetBrains Mono cells. The model emits in the β̂ row (same 11pt, same monospace, distinguishable only by position). The author emits the five chapter titles and three aphorisms in DM Serif Display; those three are the only allowed serif appearances in the site. The reader's interactions are: edit the `p` cell, edit the `α` cell, edit `fold` cells, double-click to commit in CH1, click `[object]` affordances in the β̂ row, drag-to-JOIN in CH4. Six gestures. All cell-level. All instantaneous. **No animation lasts longer than a single UI state-change frame.** The single allowed exception is the CH2 spreadsheet *buckling* — which isn't an animation but a layout consequence, and that's why it earns its pixels. DA's three consecutive ESCALATES (nine sliders → brass pastiche → cinematic confession) collapse into one discipline: the site doesn't have moments; it has publishes.
  - **Why it works:** DA has been arguing for five rounds that the committee was staging a world's-fair exhibition. This is the final commitment that we aren't. The interaction vocabulary matches the visual vocabulary matches the pedagogical vocabulary: cells, edits, emissions, and the reader's attention.
