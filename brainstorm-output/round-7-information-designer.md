### CH1 — *Norway is indistinguishable from McDonald's*

- ★ **The `origin` Column Is Where Role-Color Comes From** *(amplify: DA R6 ESCALATE — derive the color taxonomy on camera + mashup: Aesthete's typeset CSV + IxD's Query Bar + my null-column)*
  - **What:** The CSV ships with four sticky columns at the far left: `iso`, `gdp_per_capita`, `r_null_95pct`, and — new — `origin`. Every one of the 356 features has an `origin` value: `WB_growth_benchmark` (features that appear in Acemoglu / Barro / Solow-style causality studies — stroked green), `WB_indicator` (features scraped from the World Bank catalog with no published causal claim — stroked gray), or `authored_by_this_site` (features we synthesized — `scrabble_letter_value_of_capital_name`, `name_numerology_score`, `mcdonalds_per_million` — stroked red). **The color isn't a verdict. It's a pointer to a cell you can read.** Hovering any column header surfaces its origin cell and a one-line citation (`Acemoglu, Johnson, Robinson 2001` / `World Bank WDI 2018` / `this site; see /construction`).
  - **Why it works:** Answers DA's ESCALATE squarely — the taxonomy is derived on-screen, in the dataset's own grammar, with cited provenance. Role-color stops being authorial fiat and becomes a derived property of a column the reader can query, sort, and (in the X-cut below) overrule. The site no longer teaches humility in a confident palette; it teaches humility with a palette that documents its own confidence.
  - **Risk / prerequisite:** Real citations required — fabricating an `origin` value is worse than not having the column. `authored_by_this_site` must be honestly labeled; we *did* construct the Scrabble feature, so we say so.

- ★ **The Commitment Is a Bet About the `origin`** *(amplify: DA R4 AMPLIFY Norway-structural + DA R6 ESCALATE contestable labels + mashup: Pedagogue's commit-about-Norway + IxD's Double-Click Commit)*
  - **What:** After the query bar, the forced choice: two anonymized column headers, both showing `r = 0.78` against Norway-adjusted GDP, both with Norway's raw value visible in her row (`column_A[NOR] = 9.4`, `column_B[NOR] = 14.0`). On reveal: both names fill in AND both `origin` values fill in — `rule_of_law_idx` (`WB_growth_benchmark`, green) and `scrabble_letter_value` (`authored_by_this_site`, red). The reader's pin persists in Norway's row. **The commitment is now a commitment about provenance, not about intuition.**
  - **Why it works:** Binds DA's two Round 6 provocations in one gesture. The reader stakes Norway's wealth on an `origin` they can't see, then watches the `origin` column reveal itself. The color grammar the rest of the site uses is now the grammar of the reader's own first-second's decision.
  - **Risk / prerequisite:** Both origins must be visually hidden during the commit window, including in any tooltip. If the reader picks correctly, the pin is green (predicted provenance right); if not, red; CH4's redemption copy branches accordingly.

### CH2 — *Norway's prediction explodes*

- ★ **Norway's Leverage Is the Quiet Scalar** *(amplify: DA R5 AMPLIFY CSV buckles + mashup: IxD's Column-Adding + Aesthete's buckling sheet + my leverage-as-cell)*
  - **What:** As the buckling unfolds, three cells quietly update in Norway's sticky row alongside `gdp_predicted` and `pi_95%`: `h_ii` (leverage), `log(λ_min(XᵀX))` (pinned to the margin, not Norway's row), and `fraction_of_model_that_is_Norway`.   - **Why it works:** DA's buckling concession stands — the crash is the sheet failing.   - **Risk / prerequisite:** `h_ii → 1` for Norway as p → n must be verified (it does, by linear algebra, but the exact trajectory must be computed). Pinned `log λ_min` cell stays unstyled — 9pt mono, corner, no decoration.

- ★ **The Scrollbar Is the Chart, the Eigenvalue Is the Cause** *(amplify: Pedagogue's Scrollbar-As-Hockey-Stick + mashup: my eigenvalue sparkline + IxD's p-Cell + DA R4 ESCALATE)*
  - **What:** No separate figure. The browser's horizontal scrollbar at the sheet's bottom carries a 1px gold line alongside its rail plotting test-R² at every width. ### CH3 — *Norway is saved, wrongly* (answer to DA R6 KILL)

At α=0: the cell spans the full viewport, red fill dominates. At CV-optimal α: the cell shrinks to 62px wide (triumph), and its fill is *still* 83% red — segments labeled `numerology`, `mcdonalds`, `scrabble`, `beer_wine` in 7pt mono, a thin green sliver at the right labeled `rule_of_law`. **The narrowness and the red-ness are the same cell.** There is no headline and no footnote. There is one 62px rectangle in which the triumph (width) and the trap (composition) are inseparable. One mono line beneath the sheet: `Norway's prediction is narrow. 83% of the narrowness is red.`
  - **Why it works:** DA demanded a CH3 signature moment. This is it. Width does what the narrow-PI headline used to do; fill does what the museum-label footnote used to do; they occupy the same 62 pixels and cannot be visually separated. The reader who glances sees a short red bar and knows everything. The reader who lingers reads the segment labels and learns which absurdities did the narrowing. Glance and dwell return the same verdict. This is the pixel DA asked for.
  - **Risk / prerequisite:** Segment widths must be real (|β_j · x_NOR,j| normalized). Role-color on segments is the first time fills appear in the site's stroke-only grammar — this is the *one allowed exception*, because the composition bar is the one chart where fill-color is load-bearing. Aesthete and Interaction Designer have been warned. 7pt mono segment labels must stay legible; if two segments are too narrow for labels, labels float above the cell.

### X-CUT — Cross-cutting

- ★ **The `origin` Column Is Queryable AND Editable** *(amplify: DA R6 ESCALATE — defend the color labels or hand them over; we hand them over)*
  - **What:** The `origin` column is the site's provenance grammar — three values (`WB_growth_benchmark`, `WB_indicator`, `authored_by_this_site`), each derived from a cited rule. Role-color is bound to `origin` via the rule: green = benchmark, gray = indicator, red = authored. **Hovering any `origin` cell surfaces its citation in 9pt mono** (`Acemoglu et al. 2001, Table 4`; `WB-WDI indicator code SI.POV.GINI`; `constructed for this site; see /construction/{feature_name}.py`). **Clicking an `origin` cell opens an edit affordance**: the reader can reassign the cell's value (e.g., move `mcdonalds_per_million` from `authored` to `WB_indicator`, arguing it's a real observation we didn't fabricate).   - **Why it works:** DA's ESCALATE in full. The authorial palette is not hidden, not defended, not imposed — it is a column the reader can read, query, cite, and overrule.   - **Risk / prerequisite:** Reconciliation must be O(1) — all role-strokes in the DOM bound to the `origin` value via a single CSS custom property per feature, so an edit propagates instantly. The closing footer link to the exported diff is the one concession to shareability.
