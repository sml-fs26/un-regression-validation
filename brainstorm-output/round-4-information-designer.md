## Round 4 · Cross-pollination · Information Designer

### CH1 — The Bait

- ★ **The Spreadsheet Opens** *(mashup: DA's ESCALATE "use the dataset as material" + my ECDF-by-role + my permutation null-band + Aesthete's typographic restraint)*
  - **What:** CH1 opens on **the actual CSV** — a 254×356 monospace grid, column headers rendered at 9pt (`gdp_per_capita`, `rule_of_law_idx`, `mcdonalds_per_million`, `beer_wine_ratio`, `name_numerology`, …), row headers being country ISO codes. No styling beyond the grain of the data. On scroll, every column collapses into a single tick mark — its |r| against GDP — and the ticks rain down to form three stacked ECDF lanes (causal/spurious/incidental) with a dashed permutation null-band behind. The *same* typography survives the transition: column names become direct labels where the curves exit. The grid literally becomes its own distribution.
  - **Why it works:** Answers DA's ESCALATE with the one material we haven't used — the dataset itself. The CSV header is the Atlas's native typography; collapsing 356 columns into a distribution is the exact gesture high-dim regression performs. No brass, no tombstones, no drawer. The data is the material.
  - **Risk / prerequisite:** Column names must stay legible at 9pt mono; the tick→curve FLIP needs per-column identity preservation (each tick keeps a data-column attribute) so direct labels land correctly at the curve tails.

- ★ **The First Whisper of the Confession** *(amplify: DA's structural-confession demand + my ECDF overlay + Pedagogue's commitment gate)*
  - **What:** After the reader's forced pick in the two-silhouette commit, one mono line types itself at the bottom of the viewport — not yet a confession, just a fragment: `> model.coef_[mcdonalds_per_million] = 0.31   [95% CI: 0.19, 0.44]`. It holds for 3s, then the viewport scrolls on. The reader will hear this voice four more times before CH4. Every return appends a new line; the confession is being *written as the reader reads*.
  - **Why it works:** DA's right — the confession is the one cross-agent convergence worth making structural. Starting it as a single whispered line in CH1 (with its honest bootstrap CI attached from the start, per my error-bars-mandatory rule) means CH4's full indictment is not a new form but a completion. The reader has been watching the transcript accumulate without noticing.
  - **Risk / prerequisite:** One line per chapter, same typography, same CI bracket grammar. Breaking the rule once kills the effect.

### CH2 — The Crash

- ★ **The Detaching Column** *(mashup: DA's ESCALATE "dataset as material" + Aesthete/IxD's coefficient storm + my eigenvalue-sparkline mechanism + the structural confession)*
  - **What:** The storm is not pins on an abstract rail — it's **columns tearing loose from the CSV itself**. The grid from CH1 returns. As the reader drags `p/n`, columns in the header row begin to tremble; the one tied to the smallest eigenvalue of XᵀX tears loose first, streaks upward, and leaves behind a scar where its header used to sit. The torn column's name types into the margin as the second confession line: `> model.coef_[beer_wine_ratio] = -2.7e4   [95% CI: -infinity, +infinity]`. The CI is *wider than representable*. Accumulated scars form the negative-space test-R² curve, per DA's R2 amplify — but now the debris is **named columns from the real dataset**, not anonymous pins.
  - **Why it works:** Fuses the dataset-as-material demand with the storm-replaces-hockey-stick demand and advances the structural confession. The eigenvalue sparkline supplies the mechanism (why *that* column goes first). Every tear is a named, sourced feature from the actual UN data — debris you can read.
  - **Risk / prerequisite:** Column-header typography must stay pinned; tear order must be real eigenvector output, not choreographed. The "CI wider than representable" is an honest statement about OLS past p=n — but the rendering must convey that this is math, not theatre.

- ★ **The Per-Country Ribbon, Sparse Small Multiples** *(mashup: my per-country prediction-interval scream + the anchor-4 X-cut + DA's "kill the cemetery" — replace graveyard with accumulating ribbon)*
  - **What:** Four tall narrow panels, sparse gridlines only, for Burundi / Vietnam / Poland / Norway. Each panel: x = p, y = predicted log-GDP (dot), with 95% PI as a single gold hairline envelope (per the X-cut uncertainty-as-hairline rule). As p grows, the dots stay near truth; the envelopes inflate at different rates. Burundi's envelope spans the full GDP range by p≈150; Norway holds until p≈220 then blows out. **No tombstones. No graveyard.** The envelopes *are* the mortality of the prediction, measured in log-dollars.
  - **Why it works:** Kills the cemetery motif (per DA) and replaces it with the same structural lesson — predictions die unevenly — told in honest prediction intervals tied to named anchors. The envelope *is* the grief; no skeuomorph needed.
  - **Risk / prerequisite:** PI must be computed honestly (closed-form for OLS at p<n, bootstrap past); the four countries must persist every chapter; vertical panels need responsive stacking on mobile.

### CH3 — The Patch

- ★ **The 1-SE Bracket Rendered in CSV Diff** *(mashup: DA's ESCALATE + my CV-error band with λ_min and λ_1SE + my shuffled-Y ghost curve)*
  - **What:** The α sweep is rendered as a **version-controlled diff of the model's `coef_` dictionary**. Left column: 356 feature names in 9pt mono, each with its coefficient at the current α. Right column: the same list at the previous α step. Lines that became zero this step flash red and strike through (`- mcdonalds_per_million: 0.27  →  0.00`); lines that entered flash green. Below the diff, a small CV-error curve with a gold 1-SE bracket — and **a second diff column**, dashed gray, running the identical sweep on permuted-Y. At α_1SE, the real diff has retired 340 features. The noise diff has retired 338. The reader sees the near-identical book-keeping.
  - **Why it works:** Dataset-as-material via diff syntax — the reader's mental model of `git` / `pip list` / `sklearn` is recruited directly. Lasso's pitch becomes legible as *what the fit dropped*, honestly, with the 1-SE region showing it is a *range*. The noise-diff ghost is the quiet seed of CH4.
  - **Risk / prerequisite:** Mono grid at 9pt × 356 rows must be virtualized on scroll; the gray permutation diff must be unmistakably subordinate. Reader needs a briefing that `-` means "dropped" and `+` means "kept."

- ★ **The Confession, Struck Through Mid-Line** *(amplify: DA's structural-confession demand, CH3 beat + Pedagogue's False Dawn)*
  - **What:** A single mono line types itself at the bottom: `> model.coef_[beer_wine_ratio] = 0.14   [95% CI:` — and *stops*. The cursor sits, blinking, for 1.2s. Then the line is struck through as Lasso zeros the coefficient at the current α; the unfinished CI bracket is never closed. Third confession line appears: `> model.coef_[rule_of_law_idx] = 0.09   [95% CI: 0.04, 0.15]`. Honest, narrow, small. The reader feels the triumph: *the model shut up about beer*. CH4 will reveal it just moved the noise to a new column.
  - **Why it works:** The mid-line strikethrough is a language the reader knows from terminals; it delivers the "false dawn" as syntax, not as narration. The honest narrow CI on `rule_of_law_idx` *contrasts* with CH2's infinite-CI blowup — uncertainty is behaving again. That's the trap.
  - **Risk / prerequisite:** Strikethrough must apply to the CI bracket too — the line must look *abandoned*, not completed. Timing of the strike (1.2s hold) is everything.

### CH4 — The Reveal

- ★ **The Confession Completes, Stability-Annotated** *(amplify: DA's structural confession + my Confession Equation typography + my selection-stability heatmap)*
  - **What:** CH4 opens with the running transcript visible — four chapters' worth of mono lines, some whispered, some struck through, some with infinite CIs. Then the full confession resolves: 12 lines type in sequence, each with type-size scaled to |β̂|, each with its honest bootstrap CI bracket, each followed by **a 200-cell stability strip** (selected/not-selected across resamples). McDonald's has a solid red strip (198/200). Rule-of-law has a broken strip (112/200). Causal role-stroke green, spurious red, both present. The confession's punchline is no longer "look at these absurd picks" — it is "**the absurd picks are the stable ones.**"
  - **Why it works:** Completes DA's four-chapter thread. Fuses type-size-as-magnitude (my R2 idea) with stability heatmap (my R2 idea) with the full typewriter scene (Aesthete) without metaphor pastiche. Stability strip refutes the charitable read *in the same line* as the confession. The structural build across four chapters makes the resolution pedagogical, not spectacular.
  - **Risk / prerequisite:** Strip width must be proportional to CI width — two uncertainty channels at once; must verify this reads clearly in prototype. 200 bootstrap Lasso fits precomputed. The four earlier chapters' mono lines must persist as a sidebar scroll.

- ★ **The Dueling Columns, PDP-Annotated** *(mashup: Aesthete's dueling ledgers + my partial-dependence superposition + DA's ESCALATE (dataset syntax))*
  - **What:** Two narrow columns of mono rows. Left: `lasso.feature_importances_`. Right: `rf.feature_importances_`. Each row is the feature name + its magnitude + a **14px inline sparkline of the feature's PDP from that model**. Lasso's sparklines are all straight lines (they *have* to be); RF's are curves that saturate, dip, plateau. Matching features are joined by a single gold hairline across the gap; the hairline changes to dashed-amber when the sparkline *shapes* disagree (rank agreement, geometric disagreement). Six solid-gold joins. Fourteen dashed-amber dangling or crossed. The geometry of disagreement is on the page.
  - **Why it works:** No vellum, no brass — just the mono column the data actually lives in, annotated with sparklines and hairlines. The rank/shape distinction answers "importance is not a number, it is a function." Amber-dashed vs gold-solid continues the hairline grammar from the X-cut rule.
  - **Risk / prerequisite:** 40 inline sparklines need a shared x-percentile scale; hairline thickness must stay 1px to hold the grammar. Mobile: the two columns stack with joins reduced to inline markers.

### CH5 — The Trust

- ★ **Dissolution into Nested-CV Ridgeline** *(mashup: DA's KILL demand (dissolution not boxplot) + my nested-CV ridgelines + Aesthete's ghost splits + the structural confession's final return)*
  - **What:** The headline `R² = 0.52` sits center-viewport in 180pt DM Serif. Beside it, the confession's final line types in mono: `> model.cross_val_score().mean()   [reported in ch3]`. Then the numeral dissolves — pixels recruited by 200 ghost dots, the reported number becoming one gold-tinted dot in the cloud. After the cloud settles, the cloud *splits vertically* into two ridgelines stacked: **top ridge = single-split distribution (200 repeated splits, biased-optimistic), bottom ridge = honest nested-CV distribution**.   - **Why it works:** Answers DA's KILL directly — dissolution, not annotated boxplot. Adds the piece every replication-crisis explainer omits: the systematic gap between single-split repetition and honestly nested CV. The confession closes the loop: the reader has heard this voice five times; the final line is the only one with a *narrow, honest* CI — and it is smaller than CH3's headline claimed.
  - **Risk / prerequisite:** Nested CV must actually be run (real compute). The two ridgelines must share axes; the gap must be drawn and labeled in R² points.

  - **Risk / prerequisite:** Mobile collapses to drawer. The "expand to full viewport" move is one-shot at the site's end and must feel inevitable, not theatrical.

- ★ **The Dataset Is the Material — Sitewide Typographic Rule** *(amplify: DA's ESCALATE + X-cut uncertainty-as-hairline + the one-palette, one-stroke discipline)*
  - **What:** A single enforceable rule across all five chapters: **all feature names appear only in their CSV header form** — `mcdonalds_per_million`, `name_numerology_score`, `rule_of_law_idx` — in 9pt JetBrains Mono, no title-case prettification, no decorative icons, no re-labeling for readability. Headers carry underscores, lowercase, ISO codes, the works. The data's own namespace is the typography of the site. Brass instruments are demoted to at most **one** per chapter; the default material is the raw column name and the 1px gold hairline.
  - **Why it works:** DA's ESCALATE demanded a 21st-century, data-native grammar; this gives the site a vernacular even the Aesthete's museum drawers can share. Every chart label, every confession line, every direct-label on a curve comes from the CSV header. The Atlas's voice is the voice of the data, unedited.
  - **Risk / prerequisite:** One discipline, policed in code review: no human-facing feature re-naming. The risk is illegibility for first-time readers — mitigated by pairing the first appearance of any feature with its real museum-label citation (Aesthete's brass label, once, then retired).
