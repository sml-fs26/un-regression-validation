## Round 4 · Cross-pollination · Devil's Advocate

### AMPLIFY
> Targeting the **Norway through-line** (Aesthete + Pedagogue + Information Designer + Interaction Designer — all four)
> Every agent independently reached for Norway. That is the second cross-agent convergence on a spine of the site, and it is still being used as decoration — "the same dot, highlighted in gold." Make Norway **structural**. The site's table of contents is not "Bait / Crash / Patch / Reveal / Trust." It is *Norway is indistinguishable from McDonald's. Norway's prediction explodes. Norway is saved, wrongly. Norway is a Scrabble score. Norway was one draw.* Five chapter titles, one protagonist, five deaths. The reader scrolls a biography, not a textbook. And — critically — Norway is not hand-picked by the committee. Norway is chosen by the analysis: whichever country performs the five-act arc most starkly wins the role. If it's Uruguay, the site is called *Five Deaths of Uruguay*. Let the data cast its own protagonist.

### KILL
> Targeting the **Rose Window of Folds** (Aesthete CH5 + Interaction Designer CH5 + Pedagogue CH5)
> Three agents converged on a gothic rose window with painted petals. Stop. This is exactly the 19th-century-instrument pastiche I flagged in Round 3 ESCALATE, and the committee doubled down on it instead of retreating. A gothic cathedral window teaches the reader nothing about k-fold CV that a bootstrap jitter cloud or a strip of stacked bands doesn't teach faster and without the Ken Burns documentary music. "Paint your own folds onto a world map" is five minutes of cognitive effort for one lesson (stratification matters). The Dissolution is already CH5's signature moment — it does not need a cathedral wedged in behind it. Cut the rose window. What belongs in the empty slot: nothing. Let the Dissolution stand alone. CH5 earns its brevity.

### ESCALATE
> The committee has now agreed on five signature moments — drawer, storm, brass scale, confession, dissolution — and four of them are **typographic or material metaphors imported from outside the dataset**. Vellum, brass, index cards, ink. The dataset itself — a 254×356 CSV from the World Bank, with ISO-3 country codes, with column headers like `SP.POP.TOTL` and `NY.GDP.PCAP.KD`, with feature names pulled from actual UN indicator IDs — has not appeared as visual material **once** across four rounds. At least one chapter's signature image must be **the spreadsheet itself** — cells, headers, country codes, the literal texture of the data. The Confession already gestures at this (monospace feature names as prose). Push it further, or admit the site is an atlas of *a dataset it refuses to show*.
