## Round 7 · Cross-pollination · Devil's Advocate

### AMPLIFY
> Targeting Information Designer's **"Norway's Row Has Its Own Leverage Number"** (CH1, Round 6) — `h_ii = 0.31` as a sticky cell
> This is the quietest and most devastating proposal anyone has made in seven rounds, and the committee has not noticed. A single scalar — Norway's leverage — climbs from `0.04` in CH1 to `0.98` in CH2 to a narrow band in CH3 to whatever bootstrap does to it in CH5. **That number is the biography.** Make it structural: Norway is the protagonist *because her h_ii arc is the starkest over 254 countries*, the audition is computed on leverage alone, and every chapter's signature diagnostic is the current value of `h_ii` displayed in the gutter beside her ISO-3. The site's thesis — "a country becomes 98% of her own prediction" — is a one-number teachable fact that no explainer has ever shown. Promote `h_ii` from a sticky cell to the site's **ticker**: always visible, always updating, the single most honest quantity in the whole CSV. The Dissolution in CH5 is not of `R²`; it is of `h_ii × β`. The protagonist was not chosen; she was measured.

### KILL
> Targeting Aesthete's **"The Title Spread"** (CH1, Round 6) — two-page indigo spread, `I.` at 240pt DM Serif gold, page-turn animation at 900ms
> The committee just committed, in the same round, to emit-don't-perform. And the first frame of the site is 240pt Caslon gold with a perspective page-fold. No. The opera staging does not get to survive at the title card while being retired everywhere else — that is exactly the contradiction readers will feel without naming. If the confession arrives in 11pt mono without a tick, the *title* arrives in 11pt mono without a tick: `# five_deaths_of_norway.ipynb` in the same JetBrains Mono as every other cell, top-left of a CSV that is already visible. The reader is inside the sheet from scroll-zero. A book-cover title page is a publisher's reflex; this site's publisher is the dataset. Retire the spread. No page-turn. No gold numeral. The first screenshot of the site is the sheet, and the title is a filename.

### ESCALATE
> Round 6 delivered a scrollytelling defense I accept. Round 6 did **not** answer Round 6's other ESCALATE: the color grammar. Green=causal, red=spurious, gray=incidental is *authorial fiat* in a site whose entire thesis is that authorial fiat about what predicts wealth is how you end up with Scrabble scores in the top 20. The committee's color palette presupposes the authors knew — before any chapter — which features were honest and which were lies. That presupposition is the thing the site is teaching readers to distrust. This is the deepest unexamined contradiction in seven rounds. Either (a) derive the taxonomy on camera in CH1, using the site's own emission grammar — causal/spurious/incidental is a *column in the CSV*, populated by a stated procedure, auditable, contestable; or (b) give the reader a cell they can edit: click `mcdonalds_per_million`, type `gray` or `green` in its role column, and watch every subsequent chapter's verdict recompute against the reader's own taxonomy. If the site is a spreadsheet, the labels are editable. If the labels are not editable, the site is not a spreadsheet — it is a textbook in CSV drag. Pick one by Round 8.
