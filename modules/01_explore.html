CHAPTER 01 · THE BAIT

What predicts GDP?

The dataset hands you 493 columns, each labelled as one of three roles: causal (a plausible economic mechanism), spurious (correlation without cause), and incidental (a real statistic that is not a root cause). The story of Chapter 01 is that you cannot tell them apart by correlation alone. Scatter, rank, and then test yourself.

takeaway

High correlation does not mean a causal mechanism. Incidental features correlate with GDP as strongly as causal ones; the eye cannot separate them on a scatter plot. Even the strongest of 333 spurious features reaches |r| ≈ 0.47 — not because of any mechanism, but because with hundreds of shots at the target, some noise lands close. In the next chapter we hand every one of these features to a linear regression and watch what happens when we ask it to predict.
